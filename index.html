Overview

The Semantic Web was envisioned as a space where autonomous agents collaborate with humans to navigate, reason with, and act upon structured knowledge. While core technologies like RDF, OWL, and SPARQL have matured, the original vision remains unfulfilled.

Recent advances in generative AI and multi-agent systems open new possibilities â€” but challenges around semantic grounding, goal-driven autonomy, and trustworthiness remain. This workshop brings together researchers and practitioners from Semantic Web, AI, multi-agent systems, and robotics to explore how generative AI can be integrated with Semantic Web technologies to create semantically grounded, context-aware, interoperable, and autonomous agents.
